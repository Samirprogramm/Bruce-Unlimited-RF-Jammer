How the model scores a wallet

  1. Free-throw test. Each bet has a market-implied probability baked into its price. Buy "Yes" at 0.70 and the crowd expects a 70% hit — so an average bettor wins ~70% of bets like that.
  2. Skill, not luck. We compare a wallet's actual wins to that baseline with a Poisson-binomial z-test. Hit 95 where 70 was expected and you're sharp; hit 70 and you're average.
  3. 97% fail. Most wallets win at roughly their implied rate — they just size in with money and get lucky. Only ~3% beat chance.
  4. Weighted ranking. The wallet hitting 95 carries far more weight than the one hitting 75. Every wallet gets a confidence score for how far it beats the baseline.
  5. Consensus signal. One sharp wallet can be wrong. But when several of the sharpest independently agree, without talking to each other, that's the signal.
